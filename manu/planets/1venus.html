VENUS




  • Venus has a thick atmosphere.

    Venus has a thick atmosphere composed mostly of carbon dioxide with clouds of sulfuric acid, trapping heat and causing a runaway greenhouse effect. This makes Venus the hottest planet in our solar system.



  • Venus is often called Earth's "sister planet."

    Despite being similar in size and mass to Earth, Venus has a vastly different climate. It has a slow rotation on its axis and rotates in the opposite direction to most planets in the solar system.



  • Venus is the brightest natural object in the night sky after the Moon.

    Its brightness is due to the reflection of sunlight off its thick cloud cover. Venus is visible to the naked eye and is often called the "Evening Star" when seen in the western sky after sunset or the "Morning Star" when seen in the eastern sky before sunrise.
