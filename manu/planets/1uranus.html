Uranus




  • Uranus is an ice giant.

    Uranus is composed mostly of ices such as water, ammonia, and methane. It has a thick atmosphere of hydrogen and helium, but the outer layers contain more ices compared to Jupiter and Saturn.

  • Uranus rotates on its side.

    Unlike most planets, Uranus rotates on its side, with an axial tilt of about 98 degrees. This extreme tilt results in unique seasonal patterns and a peculiar magnetic field orientation.

  • Uranus has a faint ring system.

    Uranus has a system of thin, dark rings. These rings were discovered in 1977, and there are 13 known rings around the planet.

  • Uranus has a blue-green color due to the presence of methane.

    Methane in Uranus's atmosphere absorbs red light, giving the planet its distinctive blue-green appearance. This coloration is different from the predominantly reddish appearance of Jupiter and Saturn.

  • A day on Uranus is short, but a year is long.

    Uranus has a rapid rotation, completing one rotation in about 17.24 hours. However, it takes approximately 84 Earth years for Uranus to orbit the Sun once.
