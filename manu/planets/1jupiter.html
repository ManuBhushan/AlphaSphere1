Jupiter




  • Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system.

    Jupiter is a gas giant with a diameter of about 86,881 miles (139,822 kilometers), making it more than 11 times the diameter of Earth.

  • Jupiter has a strong magnetic field.

    Jupiter's magnetic field is the strongest of all the planets in our solar system. It has a large number of moons, including the four largest known as the Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.

  • Jupiter has a prominent feature known as the Great Red Spot.

    The Great Red Spot is a giant storm that has been raging on Jupiter for at least 350 years. It is so large that three Earths could fit inside it.

  • Jupiter has a short day but a long year.

    Jupiter rotates on its axis very quickly, completing one rotation in about 9.9 hours. However, it takes about 11.86 Earth years for Jupiter to complete one orbit around the Sun.
