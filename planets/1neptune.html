Neptune




  • Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun in our solar system.

    Neptune is located beyond Uranus and is considered an ice giant. It is about 4.5 billion kilometers (2.8 billion miles) away from the Sun.



  • Neptune has a storm system similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot.

    The Great Dark Spot, a storm system in Neptune's atmosphere, was observed by the Voyager 2 spacecraft. However, this feature has since disappeared, and new storm systems have been observed.



  • Neptune has a faint ring system.

    Like Uranus, Neptune has a system of rings. These rings are difficult to see from Earth and were discovered in 1984 by the Voyager 2 spacecraft.



  • Neptune has the strongest winds in the solar system.

    The winds on Neptune can reach speeds of up to 2,400 kilometers per hour (1,500 miles per hour). These high-speed winds are believed to be a result of the planet's internal heat and its fast rotation.



  • Neptune has a relatively short day but a long year.

    Neptune rotates on its axis relatively quickly, with a day lasting about 16 hours and 6.7 minutes. However, it takes approximately 165 Earth years for Neptune to complete one orbit around the Sun.
